75 books

Created Jan 22, 2014

I have a goal to read 75 books between Jan. 1st 2014 and Jan. 1st 2015.

Assigning reading goals is not uncommon, but I’ve never done it before. You see, I’ve never had trouble reading ten, twenty, thirty books a year — it’s natural. So when people assign themselves ten or fifteen books a year as a goal, I just smile. Look at me; such an accomplished reader. Who needs a goal to do what you love?

Well, accomplishment is a relative measure. Instead of comparing my reading volume to the average American (which is rather like judging yourself a good runner because you outran a corpse), I should compete with people like Michael Fogus, who read 118 books in 2013! See how even 75 books — about 1.5 books per week — suddenly doesn’t seem like very much?

There are some potential problems with such a quantitatively defined goal. After all, what about reading valuable things that aren’t books — like papers? Also, won’t I be motivated to read more time-cheap books instead of deeper books that require a large investment? Will I ever read my copy of Intro to Algorithms?

I don’t think these problems have an easy solution. But at the same time, none of them can be allowed as a legitimate excuse, or rather, as a rationalization for not reading even this basic quantity. Best is to follow the spirit of the goal — learning, thinking, diving into new material. Good is to follow the letter of the goal. Bad is to lose sight of the goal entirely and reduce my reading because of it.

Although I do have a page for my general book list, which is generated from my Goodreads account, I want to maintain here a separate list for purely motivational reasons.

Let this be a reminder that learning doesn’t stop in college.
