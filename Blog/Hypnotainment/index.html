Hypnotainment

Created Oct 14, 2013

I could say with pride that "I don’t watch TV" unlike all those other lazy loungers, and in fact I have the opportunity to do so every two weeks when Charter calls me about "bundle deals", but the truth of the matter is I have my own flavors of "hypnotainment": I watch anime, devour Youtube videos (damn you Etho, stop talking about snacks), browse Reddit, etc. So for a week I haven’t done any of those things and my life almost fell apart. Just kidding. But it was hard: if I had given myself a longer time than a week, I know I would have failed, perhaps as I sat home utterly bored on Sunday, having checked out a book from the library and then found that it wasn’t very good, or as I came home from work with no plans for the evening and no back-pocket strategy to unwind, or when my DotA 2 friends invite me to play and I want nothing more than to accept the invitation but I mustn’t.

To be more specific I followed an exact set of rules:

First Dogma
No video games. Physical games are still allowed.
Second Dogma
No video entertainment - youtube or anime or movies.
Third Dogma
No reddit, or other Internet timewasting.

It’s not about what’s being rejected. I thought there was a chance that not having easy recourse to reddit and Youtube would inspire me to be more productive, but I suspected it wouldn’t, and it didn’t. To me it’s more about the taste of discipline that comes from giving yourself an arbitrary rule and sticking to it. It’s about practising the skill of self-control, like when your nose itches but you don’t scratch it, just because.

Immediate reason is contrary to the concept of self-discipline. If you have a good reason to do something, the presence of that reason mitigates the discipline required. So I was never too concerned with the reason for taking a week off video games, movies, anime, Youtube, and all social media and BBS-style websites. I was curious to know if I could, and to see what I would do in all that spare time, and so I just did it. (What did I do in my spare time? Read. In one week, I finished three books.)

I actually kind of enjoyed the experience, although I wouldn’t say I found it pleasant. Indeed; so used to having these fun, relaxing, time consuming activities available, I suddenly found myself faced with the terror of unremitting boredom. Risking melodrama, I can say I hated it. Yet, at the same time, there was a kind of pleasure involved, like the masochistic pleasure that creeps through the pain of a hard tissue massage. Is this what people mean by "discipline"?
