Bite-size Asceticism: No Hypnotainment

The Report

I could say with pride that "I don’t watch TV" unlike all those other lazy loungers, but the truth of the matter is I have my own flavor of "hypnotainment" when I watch anime, devour Youtube videos (damn you Etho, stop talking about snacks), browse Reddit, etc. So for a week I haven’t done any of those things and my life almost fell apart. Just kidding. But it was hard: if I had given myself a longer time than a week, I know I would have failed, perhaps as I sat home utterly bored on Sunday, having checked out a book from the library and then found that it wasn’t very good, or as I came home from work with no plans for the evening and no back-pocket strategy to unwind, or when my DotA 2 friends invite me to play and I want nothing more than to accept the invitation but I mustn’t.

It’s not about what’s being rejected. I thought there was a chance that not having easy recourse to Reddit and Youtube would inspire me to be more productive, but I suspected it wouldn’t, and it didn’t. To me it’s more about the taste of discipline that comes from giving yourself an arbitrary rule and sticking to it. It’s about practising the skill of self-control, like when your nose itches but you don’t scratch it, just because.

Immediate reason is contrary to the concept of self-discipline. If you have a good reason to do something, the presence of that reason mitigates the discipline required. So I was never too concerned with the reason for taking a week off video games, movies, anime, Youtube, and all social media and BBS-style websites. I was curious to know if I could, and to see what I would do in all that spare time, and so I just did it. (What did I do in my spare time? Read. In one week, I finished three books.)

I actually kind of enjoyed the experience, although I wouldn’t say I found it pleasant. Indeed; so used to having these fun, relaxing, time consuming activities available, I suddenly found myself faced with the terror of unremitting boredom. Risking melodrama, I can say I hated it. Yet, at the same time, there was a kind of pleasure involved, like the masochistic pleasure that creeps through the pain of a hard tissue massage.

I think next week I’ll do it again, with a different rule.

The Rules

1st Dogma:

A blanket prohibition on all movies, television, anime, and YouTube entertainment.

Exception: Watching music or non-entertainment video on YouTube.
2nd Dogma:

A complete denouncement of social media, notably Reddit and HN, but extensible to any other site on the Internet that is designed for entertainment, news consumption, or BBS-esque communication.

Exception: Email. I am not an email fiend, so this only constitutes reading 3-5 emails a day and responding to a few.
3rd Dogma:

A comprehensive deportation of video games.

Exception: None.
