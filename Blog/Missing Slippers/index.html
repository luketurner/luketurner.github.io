Missing Slippers

It’s the middle of autumn. It seems like the temperature is dropping a few degrees every day. Your apartment has hardwood floors that feel like ice on bare feet, and your circulation isn’t very good down in your fingers and toes, so you’ve started wearing slippers at home. Every day you get home from work, take off your shoes, and slide on your slippers.

The floor’s worst in the mornings, so every night you lock your apartment door, turn off the light, and walk over to bed. You take off your slippers, leaving them neatly side-by-side, so you can sit up in the morning and slip them on, never having to touch the floor with bare feet.

One day, you follow the same routine: lock the door, lights off, slippers by the bed. You snuggle into your comforter and fall into a deep, uninterrupted sleep. In the morning, your alarm wakes you up as usual. The air outside your blankets feels like it could freeze you solid. As you groggily reach over to turn off the alarm, you get the sense that something is wrong. Suddenly you freeze, goosebumps creeping up your spine. Your hand is hovering over the still-beeping alarm, but your attention is elsewhere: your slippers aren’t by your bed where you left them. In fact, as you look around, you don’t see them anywhere in the room. Didn’t you leave them there, side-by-side, like you always do?

Slowly, almost unwillingly, you notice something else: your apartment door’s deadbolt lever, that you conscientiously flip to the right every night, is turned left: the door is unlocked.
